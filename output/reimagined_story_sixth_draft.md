    ## World-Building
    
    ### World Overview
    
    The year is 2077, and the global economy is dominated by a handful of colossal tech conglomerates, each a sprawling digital empire with its own "ecosystem" of platforms, devices, and services. Society is stratified by digital reputation scores, access to high-bandwidth networks, and ownership of cutting-edge AI assistants, with the most powerful individuals dictating the flow of information and innovation. This is a world where data is the ultimate currency, code is law, and intellectual property battles are fought with the ferocity of ancient wars, often spilling from the digital realm into public opinion and even physical sabotage. The general atmosphere is one of hyper-connectivity, relentless innovation, and cutthroat competition, where ethical lines are constantly blurred in the pursuit of market dominance and technological supremacy.
"Ayodhya Systems" stands as the most venerable and widely respected of these empires, founded on principles of open-source collaboration and ethical AI development, led by its legendary patriarch, CEO Dasharatha. Its influence extends across vast digital territories, from cloud infrastructure to advanced quantum computing, all built on a foundation of integrity and user trust. However, beneath the polished veneer of innovation, internal power struggles and corporate espionage simmer, threatening the very "dharma" or ethical framework that Ayodhya Systems prides itself on. The unique aspect of this world is the absolute fusion of personal identity with digital presence, where one's "karma" is literally tracked through their online footprint, and a digital exile can be more devastating than any physical banishment.
The vast majority of the population lives tethered to these digital ecosystems, their daily lives, careers, and social interactions mediated by sophisticated algorithms and AI. Startups are the lifeblood of innovation, yet also the most vulnerable, often absorbed or crushed by the giants. This creates a volatile landscape where brilliant minds either rise to unimaginable heights or are cast into the digital "wilderness" of unfunded projects and forgotten code, struggling to survive on the fringes of the mainstream internet. The pursuit of "righteous code" – software that benefits humanity and respects privacy – is a core ideal, often clashing with the ruthless drive for profit and power that defines the corporate landscape. The very fabric of society is woven with digital threads, making a clean break from the established networks an act of profound rebellion or desperate survival.
    
    ### Setting Details
    
    Key locations include the gleaming, data-fortified "Ayodhya Campus," a sprawling, hyper-secure corporate park where Ayodhya Systems houses its top developers and servers, serving as the heart of its operations and the seat of its corporate governance. In stark contrast, the "Dandaka Digital Wilds" represents the unmonitored, untamed corners of the dark web and the fringe internet, where blacklisted coders, independent hackers, and struggling indie developers eke out a digital existence, often operating without proper funding or oversight. Ravana's "Lanka Data Fortress" is a legendary, impenetrable server farm hidden deep within an offshore, sovereign digital jurisdiction, renowned for its advanced, unethical AI and vast repository of stolen data, operating beyond the reach of conventional law and guarded by layers of quantum encryption.
Technology is omnipresent and deeply integrated, from neural interfaces that allow direct thought-to-code interaction to ubiquitous augmented reality overlays that transform every physical space into an interactive digital canvas. AI assistants are sentient and highly specialized, capable of managing entire projects or even running small companies autonomously, sometimes even possessing their own digital personalities and ethical frameworks. Social hierarchies are determined by "Influence Scores" and "Network Trust Ratings," digital metrics that dictate access to resources, opportunities, and even social circles, making reputation management a critical daily concern; a low score can mean digital invisibility. Economic systems are primarily driven by data monetization, cryptocurrency transactions, and intellectual property licensing, with market cap and stock performance serving as the ultimate measures of success in this hyper-capitalist digital age. Daily life revolves around constant digital collaboration, virtual meetings, and the relentless pursuit of the next big innovation, with "digital detoxes" becoming a rare luxury for the truly privileged, often signaling a deliberate withdrawal from the rat race.

Rama’s forced resignation from Ayodhya Systems and subsequent struggle to launch an ethical alternative platform without corporate backing, effectively becoming a digital pariah, perfectly embodies this banishment.

Rama and Sita's partnership as brilliant co-creators, whose combined innovative genius drives their ethical tech venture and whose personal connection fuels their resilience against digital oppression, showcases this profound connection.

Ravana, as the CEO of a dark-net corporation that thrives on stolen IP, manipulative algorithms, and user exploitation, represents the ultimate evil against Rama's principled fight for digital justice and user autonomy.
*   **redemption**: Redemption involves reclaiming one's digital reputation, restoring trust in a compromised system, or successfully launching an ethical platform that rectifies past injustices and brings accountability to the corrupt. Sita's public "security audit" after being held captive by Ravana, where she meticulously proves the integrity of her code and her unwavering ethical stance to a global audience, serves as her powerful act of redemption, not just for herself but for the principles she represents.

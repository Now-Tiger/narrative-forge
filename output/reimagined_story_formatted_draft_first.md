## World-Building

### World Overview

In a hyper-connected near-future Silicon Valley, the world is dominated by sprawling tech conglomerates and a ceaseless churn of ambitious startups, all vying for market share, data supremacy, and the elusive "next big thing." This is a landscape where influence is measured in viral metrics, wealth in venture capital rounds, and power in proprietary algorithms. Society is stratified not by birth, but by access to bandwidth, processing power, and exclusive digital networks, with the "Founders" and "CEOs" of mega-corporations reigning as modern-day monarchs, their empires built on lines of code and user data. The very fabric of reality is augmented by pervasive AR overlays, neural interfaces, and AI assistants, making the physical world almost secondary to the digital realm.
Beneath the gleaming facades of glass-and-steel campuses and the endless flow of blockchain transactions, a fierce ideological battle rages. The established order, represented by powerful "Legacy Tech" firms, often prioritizes profit and control, wielding vast data troves and predictive analytics to shape user behavior. This era is defined by the relentless pursuit of "disruption," where innovation is both a sacred mantra and a ruthless weapon, constantly threatening to dismantle existing structures and birth new ones. The atmosphere is one of exhilarating opportunity mixed with cutthroat competition, where a single line of malicious code or a critical data breach can bring down an empire, and ethical lines are often blurred in the race for dominance.
The core of this digital civilization is its "Code of Conduct," an unwritten yet universally understood set of principles governing digital interaction, data integrity, and ethical AI development, often referred to as "Digital Dharma." Adherence to this dharma is paramount, as deviation can lead to severe consequences, including digital exile, blacklisting from critical networks, or even corporate dissolution. Yet, the temptation to exploit loopholes, manipulate algorithms, or engage in "dark pattern" practices is ever-present, creating a constant tension between the ideal of an open, equitable digital space and the reality of a profit-driven, often predatory, ecosystem. This intricate balance of innovation, ethics, and power forms the bedrock of this technologically advanced yet morally complex world.

The primary hub of power is "Ayodhya Systems," a sprawling tech campus headquartered in a futuristic Palo Alto, its iconic, multi-tiered data center acting as the digital "throne room" for its CEO, Dasharatha. This campus features self-driving shuttles, holographic meeting rooms, and vast server farms humming with processing power, all protected by state-of-the-art cybersecurity. Its rival, "Mithila Dynamics," is a leading AI ethics research firm known for its groundbreaking, unhackable encryption protocols, symbolized by its unique "AI Core" (Sita's bow) that only the most ethically aligned algorithms can interface with.
Exile in this world means being "de-platformed" and "unplugged" – stripped of corporate credentials, access to high-speed networks, and branded as a digital pariah. This "wilderness" is the "Gig Economy Wasteland," a vast network of under-resourced, low-bandwidth zones on the fringes of the major tech hubs, where freelancers and open-source developers struggle to connect, relying on repurposed hardware and peer-to-peer networks. Communication is slow, resources are scarce, and data security is rudimentary, making it a perilous digital frontier.
Social hierarchies are strictly meritocratic on the surface, with "Founders" and "Lead Architects" at the apex, followed by "Senior Devs," "Venture Capitalists," and "Data Scientists." Below them are the "Content Creators," "Gig Workers," and the "Unconnected," who form the digital underclass, struggling for bandwidth and recognition. Economic systems revolve around "tokenized assets," "data monetization," and "intellectual property rights," with "funding rounds" and "IPOs" being the modern equivalent of royal decrees. Daily life is an immersive blend of AR-guided tasks, virtual meetings, and constant digital notifications, with personal reputation scores and network influence dictating opportunities and social standing.
